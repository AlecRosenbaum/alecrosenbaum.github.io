_Finished_

This is a height marker I made for tracking the kids as they grow. I wanted something that would look nice hanging on the wall and could eventually be taken down and kept as a keepsake.

I started with a long walnut slab that had a nice live edge and some interesting character. The natural voids and curves in the wood give it a lot more personality than a plain board would.

![](10_raw_slab.jpg)

First step was cleaning up the slab and getting it ready for the numbers. This piece had some natural voids that I wanted to fill with black epoxy to match the numbers I'd be routing.

For the numbers, I used some acrylic templates. I taped them down at the correct heights (measured from 2 feet up to 6 feet), then used a router with a guide bushing to cut the numbers into the wood. The half-foot marks are just simple dashes between each number.

![](30_routing_numbers.jpg)

Once all the numbers were routed, I filled everything with black epoxy - both the numbers and the natural voids in the wood. Aluminum tape on the back keeps the epoxy from running through.

![](50_epoxy_fill.jpg)

After the epoxy cured, it was lots of sanding to get everything flush. Started at 80 grit and worked my way up to 220. Then a few coats of oil finish to really bring out the grain.

![](70_finished_bench.jpg)

The finished piece gets mounted to the wall with some keyhole brackets on the back. Now we just need the kids to start growing!
